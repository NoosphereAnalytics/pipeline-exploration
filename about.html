What is “Noosphere Analytics”?

Noosphere Analytics is a single-member LLC owned by Morgan Foster. Morgan has over 16 years of experience building software systems, starting with her time at Electronic Warfare Associates (2009–2013) and the Army Research Laboratory (2009). Her background spans data infrastructure (Facebook, Box), compiler and static analysis (Mozilla, Twitter, Google), and the application of machine learning to automate complex workflows (Google).

A notable example of Morgan’s impact is her re-implementation of Array sorting in the SpiderMonkey JavaScript engine—used by Bloomberg terminals and the Firefox web browser. This work produced a 5× improvement in performance (view the diff here). Though she also contributed many other important language features from the ECMAScript 6 Specification: see her commits. Morgan also contributed to global ECMA standard discussions—especially regarding how JavaScript parses dates (she's referenced in this proposal). Additionally, she was an early contributor to WebAssembly’s flagship runtime environment, wasmtime.

Drawing on these diverse experiences, Morgan’s focus now is a “smart data pipeline.” This pipeline leverages autonomous components to locate and process data that traditionally required human oversight—such as the varied formats and platforms used for school board documents. To learn more about our approach, please see our white paper.

Origins of Noosphere Analytics

Morgan began developing an autonomous pipeline prototype in response to local community needs. While volunteering on the board of directors for the Whatcom Dispute Resolution Center in Bellingham, Washington, she became increasingly involved in conflict resolution. During a period of heated discussions around Title IX implementations—where certain student groups felt vilified—a local attack on a student sparked her investigation:

  • What triggered these conflicts?
  • What were the outcomes?
  • How could they be resolved without further harm?

Data on these questions proved difficult to find. Rather than abandon her inquiry, Morgan drew on her background in machine learning, software engineering, and data analysis to build her own dataset. This project evolved into Noosphere Analytics, a company dedicated to solutions that can help others facing similar data challenges. By gathering neutral, transparent data, Morgan believes community discourse becomes more fact-driven, reducing hostility and misunderstandings.
